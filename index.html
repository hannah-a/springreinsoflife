Horses, Humans & Healing

We are dedicated to supporting Veterans, grieving and troubled youth with the healing power of horses.

"As an EAGALA Model non-profit, we incorporate the use of horses for therapeutic intervention towards promoting emotional growth and healing.

Our team includes a licensed Mental Health Professional and a certified Equine Specialist. Not forgetting our Horses which are selected for their capacity to work with individuals who are troubled or suffering."

Christianna Capra, CEO

Getting Started

At our farm, horses help people who are struggling simply because they are generous, compassionate and non-judgmental beings. As a domesticated animal, in return for our care they become our best friend. And at Spring Reins of Life (SRoL), they become our partners in healing those who are moderate to severe trauma survivors. Most of SRoLs clients either do not want to, or simply cannot “talk about it”.

We remain amazed every day how the HORSES provide solace, trust and peace to each person finding their own set of coping skills…all without saying a single word.

We will do a pre-screening appointment before beginning the work with the horses; we enter into this relationship with a plan in mind.

Generally EAP (Equine Assisted Psychotherapy) sessions are scheduled as a one hour weekly session for 4 to 8 weeks, however all EAP session schedules are based on the best interests and needs of the client so this may vary one way or the other.
For the EAL (Equine Assisted Learning) workshops we offer full-day, half-day, multiple full days, or weekly (3 hours) sessions which is entirely based on the needs and schedule of the client, group or organization.

Guest visits are welcomed but must be by scheduled appointment only.
Please contact us in advance if you would like to schedule a visit.

Sponsor Op-Horse!

SRoL has hosted and funded weekly group sessions serving veterans from Lyons VA Hospitals’ combat, domiciliary, women's, and substance abuse units on Wednesdays since 2012 (excluding 2020). It has been our honor to have been able to do this for so many and for so long.
The lock-downs from Covid-19 have been especially challenging for us. For the first time in a decade SRoL is without the needed funding for these OpHorse sessions.

A typical OpHorse session lasts for 90 minutes and accommodates between 6 and 10 Veterans with up to 4 horses at a cost of $700.00 per session.
